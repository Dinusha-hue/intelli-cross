IntelliCross

IntelliCross is an Adaptive Pedestrian Crossing System using IoT and Machine Learning for real-time traffic safety.

IntelliCross is an innovative smart traffic management system designed to enhance pedestrian safety and improve traffic efficiency in modern urban environments. Traditional traffic lights operate on fixed timers, often failing to adapt to real-time road conditions, pedestrian flow, or emergency scenarios. IntelliCross addresses these limitations by integrating advanced technologies such as IoT sensors, machine learning algorithms, real-time image processing, and weather-aware systems. It dynamically adjusts pedestrian crossing signals based on pedestrian density, detects emergency vehicle sirens to prioritize their passage, and extends green light durations for vulnerable individuals like the elderly or disabled. By combining responsiveness, inclusivity, and automation, IntelliCross represents a major step toward smarter, safer, and more efficient city intersections.

About System

IntelliCross is a smart, adaptive pedestrian crossing system that integrates modern technologies such as the Internet of Things (IoT), computer vision, and machine learning to improve traffic management and pedestrian safety. The system utilizes real-time data from cameras, sensors, and weather inputs to dynamically control traffic signals at crosswalks. It detects pedestrian density, classifies vulnerable individuals (such as the elderly or disabled), and adjusts signal durations accordingly to ensure safe crossing times.

Additionally, IntelliCross employs sound recognition to identify emergency vehicle sirens, granting them priority by automatically altering signal patterns. By considering real-time vehicle flow, environmental conditions, and pedestrian needs, IntelliCross enhances urban mobility, reduces wait times, and supports the development of safer, smarter cities.

Project Domain

LITERATURE SURVEY

Recent advancements in intelligent transportation systems have led to the development of various smart pedestrian crossing solutions aimed at enhancing road safety. Many existing systems rely on traditional infrastructure such as fixed-timing traffic signals and manual push-button mechanisms, which fail to adapt dynamically to real-time conditions. While these systems have been moderately successful in structured environments, they often underperform in complex urban settings where traffic flow, pedestrian behavior, and weather conditions change rapidly.


Several research efforts have explored the integration of sensor technologies and computer vision techniques to improve pedestrian detection and traffic light control. For instance, systems utilizing infrared sensors, pressure sensitive mats, and basic video analytics have shown promise in detecting pedestrian presence. However, these approaches frequently struggle with scalability, cost, and reliability especially under varying lighting and environmental conditions. Moreover, limited attention has been given to accommodating vulnerable pedestrian groups or responding to emergency vehicles effectively within these systems.


More recent literature highlights the use of machine learning and deep learning techniques, particularly convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and object detection models like YOLO, to enhance real-time pedestrian recognition. Additionally, researchers have investigated the role of Internet of Things (IoT) devices in facilitating adaptive signal control based on environmental data. Despite these innovations, current solutions often lack full integration across multiple domains such as sound-based emergency vehicle detection, weather responsive signal adjustment, and comprehensive pedestrian profiling. This gap underscores the need for a holistic and scalable system like IntelliCross that unifies these advanced capabilities into a single, cost-effective framework.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

Urban areas are increasingly facing challenges related to pedestrian safety and traffic congestion, especially at intersections and designated crossing zones. The growing volume of vehicles and foot traffic intensifies the risk of accidents and compromises efficient traffic flow. These issues are further magnified in densely populated cities where intersections serve as critical points of interaction between pedestrians and vehicles, making it difficult to ensure both mobility and safety.


Traditional traffic management systems predominantly operate on fixed signal timings and basic sensor technologies that do not account for real-time fluctuations in pedestrian or vehicle density. This leads to significant inefficiencies, such as prolonged waiting times, poorly coordinated signal changes, and missed opportunities to optimize traffic flow. Moreover, such systems fail to prioritize emergency vehicles or address the unique needs of vulnerable pedestrians like the elderly or disabled individuals.


External factors such as peak traffic hours, public events, and adverse weather conditions like rain further exacerbate these issues. Most existing systems lack the adaptability to respond to these dynamic conditions, resulting in increased congestion, heightened safety risks, and public dissatisfaction. The absence of an integrated, intelligent system that can monitor, interpret, and react to changing urban environments in real-time presents a significant gap in current traffic and pedestrian management solutions.

RESEARCH GAP

Despite numerous advancements in intelligent transportation systems, a significant gap remains in the development of fully adaptive pedestrian crossing solutions. Many existing systems still rely on static signal timings or basic sensor inputs, offering limited responsiveness to fluctuating real-world conditions such as varying pedestrian densities, unpredictable vehicle flow, and emergency situations. This lack of real-time adaptability restricts their effectiveness in dynamic urban environments.


Furthermore, while some modern systems incorporate computer vision or IoT-based components, they often function in isolation or focus on a single aspect—such as pedestrian detection or vehicle monitoring without holistic integration. Critical features like emergency vehicle prioritization, adaptive rain-response mechanisms, and support for vulnerable pedestrian groups are rarely combined within a single, unified platform. This fragmented approach leads to inefficiencies and compromises the safety and performance of smart crossing systems.


Lastly, the high cost and complexity of deploying specialized hardware in existing infrastructure create barriers to scalability and widespread adoption. Many solutions are designed for ideal conditions and struggle with performance in adverse weather, low visibility, or crowded urban settings. There remains a clear need for a comprehensive, cost-effective, and scalable system such as IntelliCross that combines deep learning, IoT, and environmental sensing to deliver context-aware pedestrian and traffic management in real-time.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE

The primary goal is to develop a smart pedestrian crossing system that dynamically adjusts signal timings based on real-time pedestrian and traffic data. Specific objectives include prioritizing emergency vehicles, detecting vulnerable pedestrians, adapting to weather conditions, and ensuring seamless integration with current infrastructure—all while maintaining cost-effectiveness and scalability.


A key focus of the research is the incorporation of deep learning-based computer vision techniques, specifically the YOLOv8 model, to accurately detect and classify different categories of pedestrians, including vulnerable groups such as the elderly and disabled. By utilizing real-time video feeds and acoustic data, IntelliCross identifies pedestrian wait times, emergency vehicle presence, and adverse weather conditions to make informed decisions. The system then dynamically adjusts crossing durations, giving priority to both safety-critical and time-sensitive scenarios. This ensures a more inclusive and responsive traffic management approach that can adapt to the diverse and evolving needs of modern cities.


Furthermore, this research seeks to ensure the system's scalability, cost-effectiveness, and ease of integration with existing urban infrastructure. By leveraging commonly available sensors and open-source AI frameworks, IntelliCross is designed to be deployable across a variety of urban settings without the need for extensive infrastructure overhauls. The long-term goal is to contribute to the development of smarter, safer cities through adaptive technologies that not only improve traffic flow and pedestrian safety but also support sustainability and resilience in urban planning.

METHODOLOGY

The methodology adopted in the development of IntelliCross centers on a modular and sensor-integrated approach, combining state of the art machine learning techniques, real-time data acquisition, and Internet of Things (IoT) components. The system architecture is composed of several interconnected modules: real time pedestrian and vehicle detection, emergency vehicle sound recognition, weather aware signal control, and adaptive traffic light management. Each module is designed to function both autonomously and collaboratively within a centralized decision-making unit, enabling dynamic and context aware signal control at pedestrian crossings.


To detect and classify pedestrians and vehicles, the system utilizes the YOLOv8 (You Only Look Once) object detection algorithm, fine-tuned with a custom dataset that includes labeled images of common urban objects such as people, cars, motorcycles, buses, and emergency vehicles. Separate detection models are implemented for pedestrians and vehicles, with a dedicated module trained using the Roboflow Vulnerable People Detect dataset to recognize elderly individuals, children, and people with disabilities. These deep learning models are optimized using GPU-accelerated training and enhanced through data augmentation techniques like scaling, rotation, and brightness adjustment, improving their generalizability to real-world conditions. The output from these models directly influences the crossing signal timing, adjusting it based on real-time pedestrian density and vulnerability level.


Emergency vehicle prioritization is managed through audio signal processing. Sound sensors positioned near intersections continuously monitor ambient noise and detect emergency sirens using Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs) as the key audio features. These features are processed by a Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) neural network, which has been trained to classify emergency sirens with high accuracy, even in noisy urban environments. When an emergency vehicle is detected, the system immediately extends the pedestrian green signal and adjusts traffic light cycles to facilitate the rapid and unobstructed passage of the vehicle. Once the vehicle has cleared the intersection, normal signal operations resume automatically, minimizing disruption to the broader traffic system.


Weather-aware control is another critical component, designed to enhance pedestrian safety during adverse environmental conditions. IoT-enabled weather sensors continuously collect data on precipitation, temperature, and visibility. When conditions such as rain or fog are detected, the system dynamically modifies traffic signal durations for instance, extending green light intervals for pedestrians to account for slower movement. This feature integrates with the core decision engine to update crossing logic in real time, balancing traffic flow efficiency with safety imperatives. The overall system is built to be scalable and cost effective, utilizing commercially available hardware and open-source software libraries to ensure easy deployment across diverse urban environments.
